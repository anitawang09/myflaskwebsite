Greenhouse Gas Emissions Accounts

The indicators in this category examine greenhouse gas emissions from economic activity

Greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions play a crucial role in climate change, as they trap heat in the atmosphere and contribute to global warming. Understanding and monitoring these emissions is essential for policymakers, researchers, and businesses striving to reduce environmental impact and transition toward a sustainable future.

Data Sources & Purpose

This website provides comprehensive GHG emissions data using the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting (SEEA) framework. It offers both annual and quarterly insights into emissions generated by economic activities across different regions. The data is sourced from international organizations such as the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC), the International Energy Agency (IEA), and the Emissions Database for Global Atmospheric Research (EDGAR).

By making these datasets accessible, the platform supports evidence-based decision-making and fosters international collaboration in the fight against climate change. üåç

    Nitrous oxide is produced through the large-scale use of commercial and organic fertilizers, fossil-fuel combustion, nitric-acid production, and biomass burning.

Climate Impact

The increasing concentration of greenhouse gases in our atmosphere is directly linked to global climate change. Effects include rising temperatures, sea level rise, and more frequent extreme weather events.
